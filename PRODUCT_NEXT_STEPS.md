## Next Best Improvements

### 1. Add emotional temperature

Field:

- Mild frustration
- Pretty upset
- Really angry
- Scared/nervous
- Sad/disappointed

Why: emotional intensity changes wording more than generation label.

### 2. Add message length control

Field:

- One-line text
- Short paragraph
- Full conversation starter

Why: teens often need shorter output; parents may need a fuller in-person script.

### 3. Add region/accent later

Not now, but later:

- US
- UK
- Canada
- Australia
- International English

Why: English-speaking families do not all sound the same.

### 4. Add example packs

Create content packs:

- Phone & social media
- School/future pressure
- Dating and relationships
- Mental health disclosure
- Strict parents
- Teens who shut down

### 5. Human validation

Test with:

- 5 teens/young adults
- 5 parents
- 1 family therapist or school counselor

Goal: find lines that sound fake, too formal, too weak, or too intense.

## Product Positioning Reminder

Do not market as therapy.
Do not market as “talk like Gen Z.”
Market as:

**A calmer way to say hard things across generations.**

or

**Helping families turn reactive messages into conversations that can actually be heard.**
